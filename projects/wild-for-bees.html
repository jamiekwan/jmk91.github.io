Rapidly facing population decline, the bee hotels strengthen the urban ecology by providing nesting spaces for solitary bees. For the Wild for Bees campaign, the structures are built entirely from recycled urban material, while creating a site-specific aesthetic for an educational initiative centered on pollinator health. We wanted to show that bee habitats can be beautiful and easy to build in your own backyard!

As part of the SUSTAINABLE.TO team, I managed the design, build, and installation of the five bee hotels. Most structures were built in collaboration with youth from at-risk neighbourhoods, and travelled to local farmer's markets and neighbourhood events (100in1Day) as part of the educational campaign. In some cases, the construction was integrated into a community workshop to invite the public into the campaign.

S.TO was named the 2014 Pollinator Advocate for Canada, and a subsequent project, DIY Backyard Bee Hotels, was one of two winners of the David Suzuki Foundation's Homegrown National Park Design Challenge.

Project Details

  • Completed while employed at SUSTAINABLE.TO Architecture + Building
  • Team: P.DOWSETT (Principal), A.STIFFMAN, J.ANDERSON
  • Locations: Fairmont Royal York Hotel, Toronto Botanical Gardens, Eastview Pollinator Park, Kortright Centre for Conservation, Blackcreek Community Farm, and Fort York National Historic Site
  • All building material was sourced locally as urban waste
  • Built in collaboration with youth participants of the Dixon Hall Mill Centre.
